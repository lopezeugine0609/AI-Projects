# Operations Automation Roadmap

These projects are designed as practical engineering case studies. Each one starts with a real workflow problem, then shows how automation can reduce manual effort, improve response speed, and make work easier to track.

## Recommended Priority

1. `lead_intake_automation/`
   - Problem: inquiries arrive from forms, calls, and messages with no consistent qualification process.
   - Solution: score the lead, identify needs, create a CRM-ready summary, and draft the first response.
2. `inbox_triage_automation/`
   - Problem: important emails get buried with billing, scheduling, vendor, and support messages.
   - Solution: classify each message, assign priority, extract tasks, and prepare a response draft.
3. `meeting_action_tracker/`
   - Problem: meetings create decisions, but tasks are often missed after the call.
   - Solution: extract decisions, owners, due dates, open questions, and task board payloads.

## Strong Problem Areas

- appointment-heavy clinics and local services
- real estate and property management operations
- home service dispatch and lead response
- small legal or professional service teams
- coaches, consultants, and agencies
- ecommerce support and order operations

## How To Present These Projects

Use problem-solution language:

- "The problem was slow lead response. I built an intake automation that scores and summarizes inquiries."
- "The problem was inbox overload. I built a triage system that separates urgent, sales, billing, and admin messages."
- "The problem was poor meeting follow-through. I built a tracker that turns notes into tasks and recap emails."

Then show the output:

- CRM-ready lead brief
- priority queue and SLA
- meeting recap and task board payload
